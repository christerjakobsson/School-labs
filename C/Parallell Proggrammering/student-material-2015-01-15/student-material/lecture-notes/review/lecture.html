Lecture 14: Review and evaluation

Objectives

After actively participating in this lecture you should be able to

  1. Assess the state of your own learning.
  2. Study effectively for the exam.

Pre-lecture

Before attending this lecture, you should do the following:

  1. Thinking of the whole course, what did you like and what did you dislike?
  2. Which topics do you think you understand well?
  3. Which topics do you still struggle with?

Lecture

Introduction (5 min @ 15)

Today we will:

  • Review the course material.
  • Find and discuss the good and the bad aspects of this course.

Review (40 min @ 20)

Write a short summary of the course!

Write down which topics you understand well!

Write down which topics you still struggle with!

Summarize the general state of learning in the class.

Organize study groups with a mix of students some of whome know a topic and some who don't.

For each topic you know well, construct one or a few exam questions!

Break (15 min @ 00)

Go over quizzes togehter.

Evaluation (40 min @ 15)

Write the following on separate pieces of paper:

  • What did you like with this course?
  • What didn't you like with this course?

Execute the following algorithm:

  1. Pair up with a random class mate.
  2. Explain and compare your notes.
  3. Select from each category the note that you both can agree on the most.
  4. You may optionally write a completely new note based on your discussions.
  5. Give the discarded notes to the teacher.
  6. Pair up with another group and repeat from 2 until everyone is in the same group.

Present and discuss the final notes with the whole class.

Save all the notes for later processing.

Review (5 min @ 55)

Thank the students.

Remind them to register for the exam.

Post-lecture

After attending this lecture, the following activities are recommended:

  1. Study for the exam
  2. Quiz yourself on fundamental concepts and terminology
