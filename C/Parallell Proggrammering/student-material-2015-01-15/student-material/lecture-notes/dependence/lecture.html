Lecture 6: Data dependence analysis

Objectives

After actively participating in this lecture you should be able to

  1. Describe the purpose and context of data dependence analysis.
  2. Describe what RaW, WaR, and WaW data hazards are.
  3. Describe what true, anti, and output data dependencies are.
  4. Analyze the data dependencies of very small programs on paper.
  5. Represent the result of a data dependence analysis in the form of a dependence graph.
  6. Identify sources of parallelism from a dependence graph.
