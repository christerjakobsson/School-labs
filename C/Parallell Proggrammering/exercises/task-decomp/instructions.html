Task decomposition in the real world

Consider the following real-world scenario:

You are the editor-in-chief of a famous weekly magazine. Every week you are in charge of producing 50 pages of high-quality articles. You are the boss of a group of reporters who do the actual research and writing.

Let us consider different ways that you can partition (decompose) the work that needs to be done during the week. The aim is to allow the reporters to work more or less independently in parallel to either finish early and take a day off or produce better quality articles.

Article parallelism

Let's say you have 20 articles to write but only 5 reporters in your staff. Doesn't it make sense then to give say four articles to each reporter and have them report back by the end of the week? You could call this "article parallelism" since the articles are the source of parallelism.

Task granularity

Suppose you have to carry two boxes from point A to B.

You could either:

  1. Transport one box at a time, or
  2. take both boxes at once.

The first alternative is easier per trip since you have less to carry, but on the other hand you need two trips to complete the job.

The second alternative will take you longer per trip but you will only need one trip, quite possibly saving you some time.

What do you learn from this example?

There is a tradeoff between task granularity and parallelism. Try to formulate the reason for this tradeoff!
